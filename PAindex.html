Laura G. is a medical student at the Keck School of Medicine of USC who is passionate about mentorship, representation in medicine, and serving underserved communities.

She comes from a Middle Eastern background and has extensive experience working with underserved Hispanic communities.

Much of her clinical work has involved Spanish-speaking patients across Los Angeles County, and she hopes to continue serving these communities throughout her career.

Laura has mentored more than 15 students individually and longitudinally, and these experiences showed her how much guidance many students need when navigating the pre-med path without family connections in healthcare.

Core Values

  • Service to underserved communities
  • Increasing representation in medicine
  • Encouragement and mentorship for first-generation students
  • Honest guidance about the realities of medicine
  • Transparency in navigating the pre-med path

Why This Mentorship Exists

Many talented students could become excellent physicians, but they are navigating the pre-med path without family members in healthcare or mentors who can explain how the process works.

Laura's experiences on both sides of care shaped this perspective. While studying abroad in the Middle East, she became seriously ill and experienced firsthand how vulnerable and frightening it can feel to seek medical care in an unfamiliar environment. The kindness and reassurance of a physician during that moment left a lasting impression and strengthened her desire to pursue medicine.

Later experiences volunteering, interpreting, and working with underserved Spanish-speaking patients reinforced how language barriers and lack of access can shape healthcare experiences. These same barriers exist in education and mentorship.

This mentorship program exists to make the path to medicine clearer, less isolating, and more accessible for students who may feel like they are figuring it out alone.

Who This Mentorship Is For

This mentorship is especially intended for students who are trying to build a path into medicine without strong advising, family connections in healthcare, or clear guidance on what steps to take next.

Priority Applicants

First-generation Hispanic/Latino students, first-generation Middle Eastern students, and students with no physicians in their family will receive priority consideration.

Students Navigating Medicine Without Connections

This space is for students who feel they are figuring out the pre-med process largely on their own and want practical, honest guidance.

Students from Underserved Backgrounds

Students from underserved, low-resource, immigrant, or underrepresented backgrounds are especially encouraged to apply.

Community College and Transfer Students

Students coming from nontraditional or less-supported educational paths are welcome and encouraged to apply.

Students Interested in Service

This mentorship is a strong fit for students who want to serve underserved communities and pursue medicine with purpose.

All Students Welcome

Even if you are not in a priority group, you are still welcome to apply if this mentorship would be helpful to you.

What I Can Help With

Mentorship is tailored to where you are in the journey, whether you are just beginning to explore medicine or preparing for applications.

Choosing Pre-Med Courses

Build a strong academic foundation and think through course planning in a realistic, manageable way.

Creating an Academic Plan

Map out next steps semester by semester so you can move forward with more clarity and less guesswork.

Personal Statement Editing

Get help shaping your story, strengthening your message, and writing with more purpose and clarity.

Application Strategy

Talk through timelines, readiness, gaps, priorities, and how to think strategically about the application process.

Choosing Extracurriculars

Figure out what activities make sense for your goals rather than trying to do everything at once.

Research Guidance

Think through how to find research opportunities and how they may or may not fit your personal path to medicine.

Honest Discussion About Medicine

Ask candid questions about the realities of medicine, training, and whether this path feels aligned with your goals.

General Pre-Med Mentorship

Bring your questions, uncertainties, and goals to a space meant to help you move forward with confidence.

How It Works

Step 1: Apply

Submit the mentorship application and share your background, goals, and what kind of support you are looking for.

Step 2: Review

Applications are reviewed based on fit, availability, and priority for students who may have less access to mentorship.

Step 3: Scheduling

Selected students will receive next steps and a scheduling option for a free one-on-one session.

Step 4: Meet on Zoom

Sessions are typically 30 minutes, with flexibility to go up to 1 hour as needed.

Because capacity is limited to about 8 students per month, some applicants may be placed on a waitlist.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for mentorship?

Any pre-med student may apply. Priority is given to first-generation Hispanic/Latino and Middle Eastern students, especially those with no physicians in their family.

Do I have to be Hispanic or Middle Eastern to apply?

No. All students are welcome to apply. The priority categories simply reflect the focus of this mentorship program.

Is this mentorship really free?

Yes. Sessions are offered free of charge.

How many students can you mentor each month?

Current capacity is about 8 students per month, so some applicants may be placed on a waitlist.

Is mentorship virtual?

Yes. Sessions take place over Zoom.

How do I contact you?

The best way to get in touch is by submitting the application form. Personal contact information is not posted publicly on the website.
